Doughnuts

Description

The experiments I've done have been interesting. Flatter than I had hoped, but with a soft interior and a very thin crunchy outside. Pretty close to the cake donuts I'd hoped for.

The next batch, I'll try for 1/2 thickness, then cut. I'm thinking of something like dowels or such.

Third batch I made a full batch, using the liquid from some cans of sweet potato instead of milk.

I had to add more liquid as 1 cup left the dough very stiff. I got it to a very soft, slightly sticky stage. Rolled to 1/2 inch thick and gently cut the donuts. I baked them on oiled parchment and oiled the donuts before cooking.

This variation is much closer to the deep-fry variety. Texture is much like a cake donut. The crumb looks like I remember home fried donuts looked when I'd break one open.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 cups sifted flour
                        260 grams clean wheat kernels
                    260 grams clean rye kernels
                    ground to very fine flour
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 cup milk

Half Portions and Changes

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 1 eggs
  • 2 cups sifted flour --

    I ground 170 grams of mixed rye and wheat and added multipurpose flour to 260 grams total flour

  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • a pinch of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon allspice
  • 1/2 cup milk

Steps

  1. preheat airfryer to 425 F
  2. Add sugar to melted shortning and beat thoroughly
  3. Beat eggs and add to sugar
  4. Whisk dry ingredients together
  5. Alternately add portions of dry ingredients then milk until done
  6. Roll 1/4 inch thick on floured surface. Refrigerate if too soft
  7. Cut with doughnut cutter
  8. Fry well-spaced batches until browned -- somewhere in 5 minute range
  9. Drain and frost.

Notes

This recipe makes a very soft and tender dough that you can roll out to thickness, then cut out the donuts and holes. These were meant to float in the hot fat while they fried, but since we have to deal with gravity in the air fryer, you should be able to flour the bottoms and slip them into the basket, right?

The dough is soft enough to ooze slightly through the holes in the basket and form millions of little brown crusty nuggets that you have to remove before the experimental donuts can be released from the mesh.

I am currently experimenting with this recipe in my Air-fry tabletop oven. It's a lot smaller than our currently nonfunctional antique gas oven. It's fine for 2 person portions, so I'm pleased with it so far.

My starting recipe for these donuts was taken from an unknown cookbook, guessing from illustration style, from 1930 - 1950. My guess would be late 30's, just before WWII.
