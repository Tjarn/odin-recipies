Risen Donuts for Air Fryer

Description

These make very soft donuts with a thin golden skin. I actually never got to glaze any as the donuts and holes disappeard too quickly.

No, the air fryer doesn't make the same thing deep frying would. But taken on their own, these taste just fine, just don't have the grease that deep-fried donuts have.

Ingredients

Donut Ingredients

Proof

  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast

Dry Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 4 teaspoons non-iodized salt
  • 260 grams flour or grain finely found.
    Consider keeping to 1/2 wheat flour or
    add 2 Tablespoons gluten enhancer.

Wet Ingredients

  • 4 Tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Toppings

Vanilla Glaze

  • Powdered Sugar
  • Milk
  • Vanilla extract

Chocolate Glaze

  • Powered Sugar
  • Caco Powder
  • Milk

Cinnamon Sugar

  • Sugar
  • Powdered Cinnamon
  • Melted butter

Preparation

  1. Proof the yeast in barely warm milk with 1 teaspoon sugar dissolved in it.
    I microwave 1/2 cup milk right from fridge for 30 seconds and it's nicely warm.
    In about 10 minutes the yeast should be foaming. My oven holds 90 degrees, so
    I use it for both proofing the yeast and raising the dough.
  2. Whisk flour and salt together until nicely mixed.
  3. Mix the 1/4 cup sugar with melted butter until it is smooth. Beat in the egg and vanilla. Test the warmth, and cool if necessary to protect the yeast. Then combine proofed yeast/milk mixture with the other wet ingredients.
  4. Mix dry ingredients into wet and form a shaggy dough
  5. Knead until elastic and will window pane
  6. Let rise in oiled bowl until double volume 1/2 to 1 hour. Don't stint.
  7. Cut donuts and put on lined baking sheet. Cover and let rise again. 40-60 min.
  8. Air fry at 400 degrees for 6 minutes or golden brown.
  9. Prepare glazes and cinnamon sugar while air-frying.
  10. Dip donuts in glaze or brush melted butter on donuts and dredge in sugar.

If you can keep 'em on a cooling rack for 5 or 10 minutes before they disappear, they'll be about as good as they will get.
